## Essendon / northern suburbs IMAMI-style multidisciplinary psychology practice

**Date captured:** April 2026

**Premise:**
My wife has a PhD, specialises in Circle of Security and attachment-based work, has ISTDP Core Training Level 1, and currently contracts to IMAMI / MIWM (Maternal & Infant Wellbeing Melbourne — miwm.com.au) as their psychology arm. IMAMI runs a referral-based multidisciplinary model: GPs, paediatricians, lactation consultants, physiotherapy, osteopathy, breastfeeding medicine, and psychology all co-located.

There isn't an equivalent practice in the northern suburbs of Melbourne. Essendon, Pascoe Vale, Moonee Ponds, Brunswick, Coburg — all heavy on young-family demographics, thin on perinatal-specialist practices.

**The opportunity:**
Spin up a northern-suburbs version of IMAMI, with my wife's reputation and PhD as the psychology anchor. Her edge over government-free equivalents is credibility — parents are willing to pay for a PhD-qualified specialist with published research in the field.

**Starting moves:**
1. **Email campaign to key Essendon-area GPs** — position the practice as a new perinatal / infant mental health referral destination. Sign them on as informal referrers first, then as contracted co-located GPs later.
2. **Identify 3–5 anchor practitioners** who'd co-locate (paediatrician, lactation consultant, physio, maternal health nurse, osteo).
3. **Pilot a once-a-week clinic day** in rented rooms before committing to a dedicated premises.
4. **Use the existing IMAMI template** — their service model, flow, and referral structure are effectively a playbook (see miwm.com.au/#service for the full roster).

**Competitive advantages:**
- PhD + published research in perinatal/infant mental health
- Established IMAMI experience — knows the operational model inside-out
- Northern suburbs geography is underserved — no direct competitor running this model
- Ties into the emerging AMH Psychology brand

**Risks / unknowns:**
- Time cost on wife to run vs. clinical hours — might need a practice manager
- Conflict of interest if still contracting to IMAMI (south/east) while setting up competitor in north
- Capital required for a proper fit-out vs. stay-lean co-location model
- GP network building is slow — 12-month horizon at minimum
