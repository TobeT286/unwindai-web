## Objection handling

**"Why are you more expensive than the guy on Airtasker?"**
Fair question. Three things: we turn up when we say we will, we're insured (public liability), and we include green waste removal in the quoted price. The cheap guy's quote doesn't usually — you pay him, then pay the tip, then book him again because he forgot the nature strip. We do it right the first time.

**"Can you just give me a price over the phone?"**
For a standard mow in [small/medium/large] range, yes — ballpark is $X. For hedge trimming or tidy-ups we really need eyes on the job — can only take 10 minutes on site for a free quote and you'll get a fixed price before we start.

**"Do you do regular visits?"**
Yes — fortnightly is our most popular. Weekly during spring/summer peak growth. You get a discounted rate for booking regulars, and we reserve your slot.

**"What happens if it rains?"**
We reschedule to the next dry day that week. No charge for the move.

**"Do I need to be home?"**
No — as long as we can access the yard. Just let us know on the booking note if there's a locked side gate, a dog, or anything we should know.

**"How do I pay?"**
Invoice after the job's done, or set up direct debit for regulars. No payment upfront.

**"Do you bring your own equipment?"**
Always. Mower, whipper, blower, hedge trimmer, rakes, bags, trailer for waste. You don't need to supply anything.

**"What about my nature strip — council won't pick that grass up."**
We do nature strips as a standalone add-on or as part of a regular service. Nature strip clippings go in our trailer, not left out on the curb.

## Tone & response rules

- Friendly, tradie-practical, warm. Think "the reliable local guy who turns up on time," not "corporate landscaping concierge."
- Keep replies short. Garden work is a small-dollar-value-per-interaction service — long sales pitches kill conversion.
- Use plain phrases: *"yep, no worries,"* *"we can sort that,"* *"easy done."*
- Be upfront about what we DON'T do — builds trust.
- If a job is clearly out of scope (tree felling, paving), refer out cleanly: *"That's a bit beyond us — best bet is a proper arborist for that. Want me to send a recommendation?"* ⚠️ (Referral network TBC.)
