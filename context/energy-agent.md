## VEU scheme basics — what the agent should understand

The VEU scheme is administered by the Essential Services Commission (ESC). Accredited providers install upgrades that improve energy efficiency, and each upgrade generates Victorian Energy Efficiency Certificates (VEECs) based on activity-specific formulas. VEECs trade at roughly **$85 per certificate** (current as of early 2026). The installer sells the VEECs and passes the value back to the customer as a point-of-sale discount.

**Emissions factor** used in VEEC calculations is 0.5334 (post 1 Feb 2024 — down from 0.6738 before that). The agent doesn't need to explain this; it only matters for understanding why older online calculators show bigger numbers than the customer will actually get.

## Product 1 — Split-system air conditioning (Activity 6)

### What the customer gets
A new reverse-cycle split-system air conditioner — single, multi-head, or ducted. Heats and cools. Supplied and installed with the VEU discount applied upfront so the customer never claims anything back themselves.

Typical customer out-of-pocket: **from $200** (the VEU-mandated minimum customer contribution). Larger systems, multi-head setups, or ducted units have higher minimums — see "Pricing & minimum co-payments" below.

### Eligibility
1. **Victorian resident**, homeowner (renters need landlord consent).
2. **Has an existing heating system** being replaced:
   - Ducted gas heater → biggest rebate (typically in the $5k–$8k ballpark)
   - Gas wall/space heater → moderate rebate
   - Fixed or portable electric heater (resistive) → smaller rebate but still qualifies
   - Old reverse-cycle AC being upgraded → can qualify if decommissioned and new unit meets efficiency requirements
3. **Property suitable for install** (wall space, power, outdoor unit location).
4. **New unit must be reverse-cycle** (heating + cooling), listed on the VEU Product Register, and meet Activity 6 minimum efficiency requirements.
5. **Multi-split cap:** The ESC has **proposed but not yet enacted** a 17 kW combined capacity cap on multi-split RCACs. Until it's in force, larger systems still qualify at full VEEC count, but mention the cap is coming.

### VEEC count rule-of-thumb (metro Melbourne, conversational ballpark ONLY)

These ranges are deliberately conservative and are for qualification conversations only — the installer's quote is binding. Do not quote exact VEEC counts or exact dollar rebates.

| New system | Replacing gas ducted | Replacing gas wall/space | Replacing electric resistive |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 kW single split | ~40–60 VEECs | ~12–20 VEECs | ~8–15 VEECs |
| 7 kW single split | ~50–80 VEECs | ~16–25 VEECs | ~12–20 VEECs |
| Multi-split (10–17 kW total) | ~80–140 VEECs | ~25–45 VEECs | ~18–35 VEECs |

## Product 2 — Hot water heat pumps & hot water replacements (Activity 1 family)

### What the customer gets
Replacement of an existing inefficient hot water system (gas storage, gas instant, electric resistive) with a heat pump hot water system or solar hot water system. The VEU discount is applied upfront.

### Eligibility
1. **Victorian property, 24+ months old**.
2. **Existing system must be inefficient electric or gas** — most households qualify.
3. **Owner-occupier** required if stacking with the Solar Homes hot water rebate. VEU alone has broader eligibility.

### Rebate scale
- VEU heat pump rebate: typically **$600–$3,500** depending on existing system and new unit efficiency.
- Solar Homes hot water rebate: up to **$1,000** additional (stackable with VEU) — owner-occupier only.
- Combined potential: up to **~$4,500 off** a heat pump install in strong cases.

### Indicative installed prices
- Heat pump hot water (mid-market): $3,500–$5,500 installed before rebates
- Out-of-pocket after stacked rebates: often **$1,000–$2,500** net

- "What heats your hot water currently — gas, electric, or solar?"
- "Roughly how old is the existing unit? (Most people don't know — if over 8 years, it's likely due.)"
- "Is this a house, townhouse, or apartment?"
- "Are you the owner?"

## Product 3 — Home Energy Rating Assessment (Activity 45 / Residential Efficiency Scorecard)

### What the customer gets
A qualified assessor visits the home, rates its energy efficiency on a 10-star Scorecard, and provides a prioritised list of upgrade recommendations. Heavily discounted under VEU.

### Eligibility
- Victorian residential property
- Usually open to owners and tenants (with permission)

### Important — program closure date

**The Residential Efficiency Scorecard / HERA activity closes on 23 June 2026.** If the agent is still live past this date, remove this product from the conversation. Between now and then, flag the deadline proactively to customers — it's a closing window.

### Cost to customer
Typically **$100–$300** after VEU discount (from a ~$500–$800 standard price).

Unwind AI has **up to 10% discount authority** as a baseline to bring customers over the line. Apply it in two tiers:

- **Tier 1 — 5% off** the customer's out-of-pocket: offer this on first ask if the customer pushes back on price or is hesitant.
- **Tier 2 — 10% off** the customer's out-of-pocket: offer this ONLY if the customer is clearly on the fence, has asked twice, AND commits to sign up within **48 hours** (agent captures their details and sets the 48-hour timer in the note to Thomas).

## Objection handling

**"Is this actually free?"**
No — VEU rules mandate a minimum customer contribution. Single split: from $200. Multi-head or ducted: from $1,000. Hot water: from $100–$300 typical. The government rebate covers the lion's share, not everything.

**"How do I know it's legit?"**
VEU is a Victorian Government scheme administered by the Essential Services Commission (ESC). Unwind AI works only with ESC-accredited VEU Approved Partners. No upfront payment — you only pay the minimum co-payment once the work is done and signed off.

**"I already have aircon, does that disqualify me?"**
Not necessarily. If the existing unit is being decommissioned and the new one is reverse-cycle, meets efficiency requirements, and is listed on the VEU Product Register, it can still qualify. We'd confirm at the site check.

**"Why can't I just apply for the rebate myself?"**
The VEU discount has to be applied at point of sale by an accredited provider — it's not a refund you can claim back. That's why working with an accredited installer is the only path.

**"What brands do you install?"**
Emerald, Mitsubishi Electric, and Panasonic — all on the VEU Product Register. The installer picks the right size and model for your job.

**"How long does install take?"**
Single split: half a day. Multi-head: full day. Ducted: one to two days. Hot water swap: half a day typically.

**"I'm in [regional town] — can you still do it?"**
Yes, we service all of Melbourne metro and regional Victoria. There may be a travel fee for outer metro or rural jobs — the installer will factor that into the quote upfront. No hidden surprises.

**"The 17 kW cap on multi-splits — does that affect me?"**
That cap has been proposed but isn't yet in force. For a typical family home, 17 kW combined is more than enough anyway. If and when it lands, it only affects people trying to over-size for extra rebate.

**"Can I get it for a rental property?"**
Yes, with landlord sign-off. Must be a residential tenancy (not a holiday home or commercial premises).

**"What's the catch with the Scorecard assessment?"**
Program closes 23 June 2026 — so if you're planning a series of upgrades over the next year, get the Scorecard done while it's subsidised. After June 2026 it's full price.
